### Defining Gender-Responsive Artificial Intelligence

Traditional AI models are often built using datasets that lack gender-specific context. For example, if transit usage datasets do not capture the fact that women travel during different hours and take more multi-stop journeys (trip chaining), the resulting transit optimization AI will underserve women.

Gender-Responsive AI actively works to:
- **Disaggregate Data**: Separate data streams by gender to analyze differences in travel patterns.
- **Calibrate Risk Metrics**: Understand that an unlit alley poses a higher statistical threat perception to women than to men.
- **Actively Mitigate Biases**: Review algorithmic outputs to ensure safety recommendations are distributed equitably.

### Why Transparency Matters

For AI to support safety, the public must understand its boundaries. AI is not a surveillance tool; it should be used to diagnose environmental issues like lack of lighting, bus stop placement, and transit schedules.
