### Why Street Lighting is More Than Just Lumens

Effective street lighting is one of the most cost-effective ways to improve safety and comfort for women walking at night. However, lighting design is often misunderstood. Simple brightness is not enough; we must evaluate:

1. **Uniformity**: High contrast between bright spots and dark shadows creates blind spots and increases anxiety. Uniform lighting distribution is key.
2. **Color Temperature**: Warm-white light (3000K) offers better color rendering and visibility than high-pressure sodium (orange) light.
3. **CRI (Color Rendering Index)**: Higher CRI helps pedestrians identify faces and vehicles clearly.

### Community Action Steps

If you notice a dark zone on your daily commute, you can take action:
- Report the specific pole identifier using the NariRaksha feedback portal.
- Engage with local resident groups to petition for pedestrian-scale lighting (lower poles that illuminate sidewalks, not just roads).
- Advocate for smart streetlights that increase brightness when pedestrian motion is detected.
